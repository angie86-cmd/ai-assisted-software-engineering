# Passport Guidance Context

## Purpose

This grounding file provides general context for passport-related guidance.

The information is intended for educational and informational purposes only.

It does not replace official sources, legal advice or consular instructions.

## General Passport Guidance

Passport processes depend on the country of origin, country of residence, age of the applicant, consular availability and applicable official procedures.

Users should always verify current information through official government, migration or consular sources.

## Venezuelan Passport Context

For Venezuelan citizens, passport procedures are generally handled through SAIME and may differ depending on whether the person is inside Venezuela or abroad.

The process may include:

- Creating or accessing a SAIME account
- Verifying personal data
- Selecting passport type
- Paying applicable fees
- Scheduling or attending an appointment
- Printing the application form or QR code
- Attending biometric data capture
- Waiting for passport production or consular delivery
- Collecting the passport

## Common Document Types

Depending on the situation, users may need:

- Venezuelan identity card
- Previous passport, if available
- Printed application form with QR code
- Proof of payment
- Police report in case of loss or theft
- Birth certificate for minors
- Authorization from parents or guardians for minors, when applicable
- Consular payment, where applicable

## Common User Risks

Users may face problems such as:

- Outdated official information
- Unclear consular procedures
- Lack of printed forms
- Missing QR code
- Incorrect personal data
- Payment issues
- Expired documents
- Lost or stolen passports
- Lack of nearby consular services
- Delays in diplomatic pouch delivery

SMID Compass should:

- Provide general orientation
