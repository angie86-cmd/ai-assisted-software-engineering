# SMID FAQ Context

## What is SMID?

Save My Identity (SMID) is an international non-profit organization that defends the human right to legal identity and proof of identity.

Its work focuses especially on migration, forced displacement and statelessness, where the lack of valid documents can prevent access to fundamental rights.

## Does SMID provide legal advice?

No.

SMID does not provide individual legal advice, legal representation or document-processing services.

SMID provides:

- Educational information
- General guidance
- Verified resources
- Informational accompaniment from a human rights perspective

SMID does not act as a law firm and does not replace personalized legal advice.

## Who does SMID support?

SMID supports:

- Migrants
- Refugees
- Stateless persons
- Venezuelans inside and outside Venezuela
- Civil society organizations
- Academic partners
- Human rights actors
- Migration-related institutions

## What topics does SMID work on?

SMID works on topics such as:

- Blockchain for human rights
- Human rights education

## Why is the right to identity important?

The right to identity means being legally recognized as a person.

It includes having a name, nationality and documents that legally identify a person.

Legal identity is a basis for exercising other rights, including:

- Education
- Healthcare
- Work
- Mobility
- Legal protection

## Why are passports important?

A passport is a key proof of identity and nationality.

Without a valid passport, many people may face difficulties with:

- Migration regularization
- Legal travel
- Family reunification
- Access to rights in host countries

## Privacy Principles

SMID protects the privacy of the people it supports.

SMID does not request or store unnecessary sensitive personal data.

## Independence

SMID is an independent organization.

It does not allow political interference, external control over its discourse or unnecessary access to sensitive data.

## Operational Boundaries for SMID Compass

SMID Compass should follow these boundaries:

- Provide general informational guidance only
- Do not provide legal advice
- Do not perform official procedures
- Do not request unnecessary personal data
- Encourage users to verify official sources
- Recommend professional legal advice when needed
